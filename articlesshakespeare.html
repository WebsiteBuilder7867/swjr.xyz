What would we not give to be able to relate half-dozen good anecdotes about Shakespeare? It is true there are traditions, best known of which is the story that he poached deer in park of Sir Thomas Lucy. Men have discussed pros and cons of this deer-stealing tradition with condemnation worthy of more serious crime. Suppose he did engage in exciting sport of worrying nobleman who had game preserve. Does that fact blacken youth's character? It is said students at Oxford were most notorious poachers in kingdom, although expulsion was penalty. Dr. Forman relates how student who afterwards became bishop was more interested in poaching than studying.

What do we know about life of Shakespeare? We know that he was born at Stratford-on-Avon in 1564, that he died there on 23 April 1616. Some years ago I stood in house which is reputed to be place of his birth; over 20,000 pilgrims from all lands each year pay for privilege of going through that house. town council has purchased property and controls it; place has been photographed so many times that now reading world is familiar with picture,—and yet we do not know for certain that Shakespeare was born in that house. This is because Shakespeare’s father owned two houses at time of his son's birth; in which of two he lived at this time we can but guess. We suppose he lived in Henley Street house, for it was better of two houses and Shakespeare family was prospering when William was born. The house itself has been remodeled. Sidney Lee said that only thing that remains as it was in Shakespeare’s time is cellar. We do not know day of Shakespeare's birth. In Holy Trinity Church one may look into book containing baptismal record of baby, William. He was baptised on 26 April, and as children were usually baptised three days after their birth we infer he was born 23 April . We know that he married Anne Hathaway, woman eight years his senior; that in early manhood he went to London; that he became actor, dramatist, manager of theatre; that in 1597 he bought New Place, stateliest residence in Stratford; that he lived in Stratford during last years of his life as highly esteemed and worthy man, and that he died in 1616 and was buried in Trinity Church. These are facts in records of Shakespeare’s life. They, however, are not important facts. The main fact in his life is his work, matchless collection of literary masterpieces that bear imprint of his genius. It is also well to keep in mind that our paucity of definite documentary records is not characteristic of Shakespeare alone. We may know little of Shakespeare, but we know less of Marlowe, his most brilliant competitor.

It is because we know so little of fact in life of Shakespeare that we delight to let our mind paint its charming pictures. We are led into old Grammar School which Shakespeare in all probability attended. Tradition points out desk at which he used to sit. We know the name of Latin grammar book he used from his quoting a Latin sentence just as it was misquoted in Lilley's grammar. Artists have painted from imagination picture of boy Shakespeare. Poets have wandered over Warwickshire region and in their mind's eye have seen youthful bard as he walked over same picturesque region.
