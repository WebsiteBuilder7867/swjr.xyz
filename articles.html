Articles

Important: whenever you use a noun or a gerund, ask yourself whether or not you should use an article.

“The,” is the definite article and indicates a specific subject or object.
This is the cat that drank the milk = we know which cat and we know which milk.

“A” is the indefinite article and indicates a non-specific, countable subject or object.
A cat drank the milk = we don’t know which cat, but we do know which milk.

The absence of an article indicates a generality.
Cats drink milk = cats, in general, drink milk.

Note; in the above examples, milk is understood to be a saucer or milk or a bowl of milk, hence countable.


Gerunds: don't usually require articles, and never use "a."
However - I like running, swimming and fishing, but its the swimming I like the most.

Countries: no article except when using the words United, Confederation or Republic, in which case use “the,” the United States, the Confederation of Independent States, the Republic of Ireland, to America, from Britain, in France etc.

Towns, cities, regions and states within countries: no article, from Queensland, in London, in Alabama.

General areas: article "the," the south of Spain, the south of Africa.

General areas when the compass points are part of the name/title: no article, in South Wales, from North America, to South Africa (the country).

Mountains: no article, on Mount Everest, the top of Mount Kilimanjaro.

Mountain ranges: article, "the," the Appalachians, the Alps.

Lakes: no article, on Lake Como, to Lake Baikal.

Seas and oceans: article "the," the North sea, the Atlantic Ocean.

Buildings etc: no article, in Westminster Abbey, in York Cathedral.

Cities: no article, London, in London.

Famous landmarks: no article, Big Ben, on Trafalgar Square, at Stonehenge.

Names of pubs:article "the," in the Lord Nelson, the Queen Victoria.

Rivers: article "the," the Thames, the River Thames.

Dates: we say "the," but we do not write it: Wednesday 11th December 2019.
