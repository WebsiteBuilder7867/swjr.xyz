Use of Tenses: Basic

Before doing this quiz, you should have studied the twelve tenses. Although students can generally structure tenses well, they often choose the wrong tense. This quiz is designed to help you choose the correct tense to use in different situations. Keep repeating the test until you get all the answers correct.

Select the correct answer. If more than one answer is correct, choose the most appropriate.

1. Action completed at a stated point of time in the past.
A) Past simple
B) Past continuous
C) Past perfect
D)Present perfect

2. Action ongoing at a stated point of time in the past.
A) Past simple
B) Present perfect continuous
C) Past perfect continuous
D) Past continuous

3. You are deciding now to take an action in the future.
A) Future simple "will"
B) Future simple "going to"
C) Present continuous
D) Future perfect

4. Action happens frequently and is annoying: used with the word "always."
A) Present simple
B) Present perfect
C) Present perfect continous
D) Present continuous

5. An action was completed while a second action was ongoing.
A) Present perfect for first action and present perfect continuous for second action
B) Present perfect for first action and present continuous for second action
C) Past simple for first action and Past continuous for second action
D) Past simple for first action and present perfect continuous for second action

6. To tell a joke, you use:
A) Present continuous
B) Present simple
C) Past simple
D) Present perfect

7. Action completed before a stated point of time in the future.
A) Future simple
B) Future continuous
C) Future perfect continuous
D) Future perfect

8. Action is ongoing at a stated point of time in the future.
A) Future perfect continuous
B) Future continuous
C) Future simple
D) Future perfect

9. Action completed before a stated point of time in the past.
A) Past simple with before, after, during and while
B) Past perfect continuous
C) Present perfect
D) Past simple

10. Action ongoing up to a stated point of time in the past.
A) Present perfect continuous
B) Past perfect continuous
C) Present perfect
D) Past continuous

11. Action completed before a stated point of time in the past.
A) Past continuous
B) Present perfect
C) Past perfect
D) Past perfect continuous

12. Several actions are completed, one after the other in the past.
A) Past perfect for the first action, past for the remainder
B) All actions are past perfect
C) Past simple for first action, past continuous for remainder
D) Past simple for all actions

13. You can see, without doubt, that something is about to happen.
A) Future with "will"
B) Future continuous
C) Future with "going to"
D) Present continuous

14. An action is completed frequently.
A) Present continuous
B) Present perfect
C) Present perfect continuous
D) Present simple

15. Action completed in the present time, emphasis on the completion of the action.
A) Present perfect
B) Present simple
C) Present perfect continuous
D) Present continuous

16. Action completed in the present time, emphasis on the duration of the action or the action itself.
A) Present continuous
B) Present perfect continuous
C) Present simple
D) Present perfect

17. Action ongoing in the present time, but not at the time of speaking.
A) Present simple
B) Present perfect
C) Present perfect continuous
D) Present continuous

18. You are asked to write a review of a new film for your college magazine. Most of it will be written in ...
A) Present simple
B) Present perfect
C) Past simple
D) Past continuous

19. You wish to express that an action completed, but do not wish to say when.
A) Past simple
B) Past continuous
C) Present perfect
D) Present perfect continuous

20. Action ongoing up to a stated point of time in the future.
A) Future perfect
B) Future perfect continuous
