IELTS Speaking Practice Tests

When doing the speaking part of the test, it is very important to use the most advanced grammar that you can. Ensure that you use a variety of tenses including less frequently used ones such as past perfect continuous, a couple of idioms or some idiomatic speech, and some phrasal verbs. Use a variety of conditionals, do your best to get a mixed conditional in. Use a variety of conjunctions and discourse markers to improve coherence. Also use some less frequently used vocabulary. If you are unsure of what to say, do not say "ummm" while you think, and remember that words are clearly separated in British English, do not join them together as you do in Portuguese. Speak at the right speed, this is about the speed that is spoken by Her Majesty the Queen. Use appropriate intonation for higher scores. Remember that you are not being examined on the content of what you say, although you must answer the question you are asked and follow a logical, sensible course. So it is OK to make the answers up provided you do not contradict yourself or say anything silly. Speaking in this way is difficult at first, but it is a bit like learning to ride a bicycle, once it "clicks," you will find it very easy.

Practice these tests on your own and then we will repeat them in class.

IELTS Practice Test 1

Part 1: 4 to 5 minutes,

How did you travel here today?

What was your journey like?

Were there any delays? Explain.

Were you here earlier or later than you expected.

Part 2: 1 minute to prepare your answer, then speak for 2 minutes. You will then be asked one or two questions by the examiner.

Do you enjoy going to the cinema?

What is your favourite film?

Which film did you least enjoy?

Who is your favourite actor and why?

Examiner: When you visit the cinema, do you prefer to go alone or with a friend. Why?

Part 3: Discussion with the examiner, 4 to 5 minutes.

Do you think that films affect the way people view the world?

Do you think that if there were less crime in the movies, people would commit less crime?

Are there any films that have influenced your life in either a positive or negative way?

IELTS Practice Test 2

Part 1: 4 to 5 minutes,

Did you enjoy your schooldays?

What subject did you like the most?

Did you have a favourite teacher? Tell me about him or her?

Did any of your teachers have a positive influence on your life?

Part 2: 1 minute to prepare your answer, then speak for 2 minutes. You will then be asked one or two questions by the examiner.

When did you last go on holiday, where to?

What did you like most about this holiday?

Do you enjoy adventure holidays, or do you prefer to relax?

Who do you like to go on holidays with, your family, friends or on your own?

Examiner:If you could go on holiday to anywhere in the world, where would you choose?

Part 3: Discussion with the examiner, 4 to 5 minutes.

What are the advantages and disadvantages of package tours?

Do you think that traveling teaches people about other cultures? Why or why not?

Do you think it is better to exchange currency in your country, or in the destination country?

IELTS Practice Test 3

Part 1: 4 to 5 minutes,

Why did you decide to take the IELTS test?

What will you do if you get a good score, what will you use it for?

Do you think it will help you get promotion in work?

What will your family say if you get a high score today?

Part 2: 1 minute to prepare your answer, then speak for 2 minutes. You will then be asked one or two questions by the examiner.

Have you ever ridden a horse, would you like to ride one?

Where to people ride horses a lot, in what countries?

Why do people ride horses in these countries, is it a part of the culture?

Do you know of any famous horse-riders, or people who are good riders?

Examiner:Have you ever seen a cowboy? Would you like to see one?

Part 3: Discussion with the examiner, 4 to 5 minutes.

What are advantages and disadvantages of going on a horse-riding holiday?

Compare riding in a horse-drawn carriage with riding a taxi, which would you prefer, and why?

IELTS Practice Test 4

Part 1: 4 to 5 minutes,

Do you come from a large or a small family? Do you enjoy doing things with your family members?

Are there any young children in your family, or do you have any young children who are close relatives?

Do you take them to fun places such as the park or swimming pool?

Do you think having children in the family makes life more pleasant?

Part 2: 1 minute to prepare your answer, then speak for 2 minutes. You will then be asked one or two questions by the examiner.

Do you drive or intend to learn?

If you could buy any car, which type of car would you buy, and why?

Do you think the speed limit for cars should be increased, decreased, or not changed?

Do you think the driving test should be made more difficult, or easier? Why?

Examiner:Should road safety and driver education be taught in schools?

Part 3: Discussion with the examiner, 4 to 5 minutes.

What are advantages and disadvantages of owning a car?

What are the health benefits of owning a car compared with not owning one.

IELTS Practice Test 5

Part 1: 4 to 5 minutes,

Do you have any hobbies?

How much time do you spend on your hobby every week?

Some people say that their work is their hobby, do you think it is true?

If you had a lot of money, which hobby would you choose to do?

Part 2: 1 minute to prepare your answer, then speak for 2 minutes. You will then be asked one or two questions by the examiner.

Some people think that global warming is a hoax, what do you think?

Do you think the effects of global warming can be felt now?

What can we do to prevent global warming?

Do you believe that pollution contributes to global warming? Why?

Examiner:Should there be more laws to force people to recycle even more?

Part 3: Discussion with the examiner, 4 to 5 minutes.

Explain your views on global warming in more detail.

Why do some countries try hard to reduce pollution and others do not?

IELTS Practice Test 6

Part 1: 4 to 5 minutes,

Do you have a job, are you in school or attending university?

Do you enjoy what you do?

If you could change your job, university or school, would you? Why?

If you could choose any job, what would you choose to do? Why?

Part 2: 1 minute to prepare your answer, then speak for 2 minutes. You will then be asked one or two questions by the examiner.

Do you enjoy walking in the countryside?

What do you like or dislike about walking for relaxation?

Do you think people should walk more? If so, why?

When you have to walk a long way, do you wear different shoes or boots?

Examiner:Do you think that people should walk more?

Part 3: Discussion with the examiner, 4 to 5 minutes.

Doctors say that we should do a 30 minute brisk walk, three times a week, do you agree?.

People walk a lot less now than 50 years ago, do you think this has a negative or positive affect on people's health? Why or why not?
