Letter Exercises

During your test, you will have little time to count the number of words, so practice writing your letters on the official answer sheet and note how many lines you use for 150 words. You can download it here: Answer Sheet. Remember that hyphenated words count as one word. You must write at least 150 words, but not over 165, complete in 20 minutes or less.

Please type your letters into Google Docs after you complete them, then send me the link for marking during the lesson. Remember to allow me permission to edit.

If you don't know how to do this, click here for instructions. Google Docs. You must have a Google account for this to work.

Remember to include lots of grammar structures, phrasal verbs, modal verbs, idiomatic speech, tenses and less-frequently used words, but in a natural way, it must not sound "forced." Remember to use an idiom in informal letters.

Practice Letter 1

Your local government is planning to cut down a very old oak tree close to your home. You don’t want it to be cut down.

Write a letter to your local council (local government) stating -

  • The reasons you do not want them to cut it down.
  • Mention public support for preserving the tree.
  • What they could do instead of cutting it down.

Practice Letter 2

Write a letter to the owner of a chicken (egg) farm to ask for a job.

In your letter:

  • State what skills you have and mention any training courses you have completed.
  • Why you have always wanted to work in a chicken factory.
  • How you will help improve productivity.

Practice Letter 3

You are doing your Bachelor’s Degree at a university in an expensive city. You have spent nearly all your money and have another three weeks until your course ends.

Write a letter to either of your parents to ask for a loan.

  • Explain why you need the money.
  • Explain why you have spent all you have.
  • Tell them how you are going to pay the money back.

Practice Letter 4

A friend has asked you to write a formal character reference letter for a charity. The name of the charity is “People Helpers United.”

  • Tell the organisation how long you have known your friend.
  • Tell them about her character.
  • Explain why his or her character is suitable for the job.

Practice Letter 5

You are planning to spend a weekend at a hotel with your family. Write a letter to the hotel to make the arrangements.

Include the following:

  • When you want to check in and check out.
  • Ask for a view with a room and and if they accept dogs.
  • Ask about tourist attractions near the hotel.

Practice Letter 6

You are taking your elderly relative on holiday, you will be staying at a hotel for a week. Your relative is diabetic and therefore has special dietary needs.

Write a letter to the hotel manager outlining her requirements.

  • Tell him about her diabetes and what could happen if she eats food with sugar in it.
  • Tell him what he can do to help her.
  • Tell him how grateful you and your relative will be if he can provide this additional service.

Practice Letter 7

A staff member of a hotel has helped you in a difficult situation. Write a letter to the manager of the hotel.

Write a letter explaining what he did. In your letter, state:

  • What was the problem was.
  • How the member of staff helped.
  • Commend the member of staff for the high standard of service.

Practice Letter 8

You recently stayed at the flat of a very hospitable Air BnB host.

Write a letter to thank him/her for such a pleasant stay.

  • Tell him that you enjoyed your time at his house.
  • Tell him why you liked staying there.
  • Tell him that you would like to stay again next year.

Practice Letter 9

You have missed an appointment with the dentist, it is your first appointment.

Write a letter explaining why you missed it.

  • Apologise for missing the appointment.
  • Explain why you still need to see the dentist.
  • Request a new appointment.

Practice Letter 10

Write a letter to your friend in another town and invite him/her to a party.

In your letter explain:

  • Why are you having this party.
  • Why you want your friend to be there.
  • Give him or her directions on how to get there.

Practice Letter 11

You recently applied to do a Bachelor Degree at a British university. One of the questions on the application form asked if you support Brexit.

Write a letter to the dean of the university.

  • Tell him that you don’t think they should ask that question on an application form.
  • Explain why applicants’ views on Brexit have nothing to do with their academic ability.
  • State that if it is not removed immediately, you will make a formal complaint.

Practice Letter 12

One of your friends recently had a birthday celebration, but you missed it and you forgot to tell your friend that you couldn’t attend.

Write a letter to your friend. In your letter:

  • Explain why you missed it.
  • Explain why you didn’t tell him or her.
  • And say what you would like to do to show that you are sorry.

Practice Letter 13

Every evening after work you visit the local pub to chat with your friends and drink a pint of beer. You went to the pub last night as usual, only to discover that they no longer sell your favourite beer.

Write a letter to the pub landlord explaining your disappointment.

  • Suggest to him that he should continue selling this beer.
  • Give him reasons to continue selling this type of beer.
  • Tell him that you and your friends will drink in another pub if the beer is not on sale again soon.

Practice Letter 14

You have bought an electric iron for your home but it doesn’t switch on. You have telephoned the shop but they will not give you a refund.

Write a letter to the manager of the shop:

  • Tell him when and why you bought the iron.
  • Explain the problem with it.
  • Tell the manager what you want done.

Practice Letter 15

You are on holidays in London, it is the first time you have visited.

Write a letter to a friend telling him or her what a wonderful time you are having.

  • Tell him/her what the weather is like.
  • What tourist attractions you have seen.
  • Why you enjoyed them so much.

Practice Letter 16

You recently met a friend whom you have not seen in 10 years while visiting London.

Write a letter to tell him how nice it was to see him again.

  • Tell him what a big coincidence it was and how much has changed in your lives over the last 10 years.
  • Congratulate him on getting married and on the birth of his second child.
  • Invite him to come with you to a football match in the near future.

Practice Letter 17

You recently ran to catch a bus which left the bus stop two minutes before it should have.

Write a letter to the bus company's customer complaints department. State:

  • Why you missed your bus.
  • How missing your bus affected you.
  • What you want the bus company to do to ensure it does not happen again.

Practice Letter 18

You have recently read an article in an international travel magazine which contained some information about your town that is incorect

write a letter to the editor. In your letter:

  • Correct the information
  • Exlain why it is important to be correct
  • Suggest what thy should do about this situation.
