Future Perfect Tense

The Future Time Period

The future simple time period is a period of time which does not yet exist.

The Future Perfect Tense

Describes an action which will have been completed by a point of time in the future.
eg. I will have lived in England for ten years on Friday.

Note: the future perfect focuses on the completion of the action whereas the future perfect continuous focuses on the duration of he action. They can usually be used interchangeably.
This tense is not used often, but it is a good one to use because it is extremely rare to hear a non-native speaker use it.

This is a general summary for revision only. For a more detailed and thorough explanation, see English Grammar in Use by Raymond Murphy or Grammarway 3 by Jenny Dooley and Virginia Evans. The first is a reference book and the second is a course book. Both are highly recommended and contain the necessary grammar exercises with answers.
