Future Simple Tense

The Future Simple Time Period

The future simple time period is a period of time which does not yet exist.

The Future Simple Tense

Describes an action which will be completed during the future time period.
If the speaker decides, at the time of speaking, to do the action in the future or is considering doing it, but has not yet made definite arrangements, we use "will."
If the speaker has already decided to perform the action before the time of speaking, or if it has been arranged before the time of speaking, we use "going to" or the present continuous for the future.

eg. I will go to town tomorrow (deciding at the time of speaking).
eg. I think I will go to town tomorrow (considering).
eg. I am going to the cinema tomorrow evening with my friends (decided or arranged before time of speaking).
eg. I am working tomorrow (decided or arranged before time of speaking).

This is a general summary for revision only. For a more detailed and thorough explanation, see English Grammar in Use by Raymond Murphy or Grammarway 3 by Jenny Dooley and Virginia Evans. The first is a reference book and the second is a course book. Both are highly recommended and contain the necessary grammar exercises with answers.
