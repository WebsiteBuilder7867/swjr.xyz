Present Continuous Tense

Present Time Period

The present time period is the time period in which we are now living. eg. now, this week, this month, this year, this century.

Present Continuous Tense

Describes an action that is ongoing during the above period of time.
eg. I am looking at enguk.ru now.

The action does not have to be taking place at the exact moment of speaking.
eg. "I am reading War and Peace now," (said while eating lunch).

Describes a temporary or changing situation. (because a changing situation is a temporary situation).
eg. I am living in Volgograd at the moment. (Volgograd is my temporary place of residence).
eg. I am working in McDonalds at the moment. (It is a temporary job)
eg. My English is improving. (Changing situation).

Describes a future action which has been agreed, arranged or decided upon before the moment of speaking. This is called the "present continuous for the future."
eg. I am working tomorrow.
