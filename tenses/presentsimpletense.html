Present Simple Tense

The Present Time Period

The present time period is the time period in which we are now living.

The Present Simple Tense

Describes a repeated action that happens regularly, often, sometimes or never.
eg. I go to church every Sunday.
eg. The bus arrives at 3.00pm (daily).
eg. The sun rises every morning.

Describes permanent states.
eg. I live in Moscow. (permanent residence).
eg. I work in a bank. (permanent job).

This is a general summary for revision only. For a more detailed and thorough explanation, see English Grammar in Use by Raymond Murphy or Grammarway 3 by Jenny Dooley and Virginia Evans. The first is a reference book and the second is a course book. Both are highly recommended and contain the necessary grammar exercises with answers.
