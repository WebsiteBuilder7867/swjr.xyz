Past Perfect Tense

The Past Perfect Time Period

The past perfect time period is a period of time which started before a point of time in the past and leads up to and includes the point of time in the past.

The Past Perfect Tense

Describes an action which was completed during the above time period.
eg. I had eaten my sandwiches by 13.00 yesterday (point of time = yesterday).

Note: The words, before, after, during and while can be used with the past simple or the past perfect, for an explanation, click here.

This is a general summary for revision only. For a more detailed and thorough explanation, see English Grammar in Use by Raymond Murphy or Grammarway 3 by Jenny Dooley and Virginia Evans. The first is a reference book and the second is a course book. Both are highly recommended and contain the necessary grammar exercises with answers.
