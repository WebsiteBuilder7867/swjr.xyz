Which and What

Which

"Which" is used for choices which are countable.

For example:

"Which doughnut would you like?" - While offering someone a choice of doughnuts.

"That is the one which I want." - While pointing to the doughnut.

When answering as above, we usually do not include "which," as it is already understood: "That is the doughnut (which) I want."

When asking about men who are unknown to you: Question: "Which man did it?" "Answer: "This (or that) man is the man that did it."

However, if you and the person you are speaking to know the men (even generally or vaguely). Question: "Who is the man who did it?" Answer: "He is the man who did it."

What

"What" is used for choices which are uncountable.

For example:

"What is the capital of Spain?" "What is the best way to do it?"

Never use "what" in the answer.

"Madrid is the capital of Spain." "This is the best way to do it."
