Idiomatic Speech Patterns.

Some idiomatic speech patters may also be classed as idioms as there is no clear dividing line between them. Remember to include one or two idiomatic speech patterns in your writing and speaking exams for level 7 and above. Do not use them in graph descriptions and formal essays. It is possible to use some in these, but knowing which ones are acceptable and which ones are not, and in what context is a native speaker skill.

You reap what you sow - what you do now will give rewards later.

Hell bent on - determined to.

Playing the game - following the rules.

Make waves - cause trouble in work.

Shoot the breeze - to relax, usually with friends.

It's just not cricket - it is not what is normally done.

Let bygones be bygones - what is in the past, is in the past, don't hold grudges.

Life's too short - don't waste time on unnecessary things, especially holding grudges.

Live hard and die young - live a wild and crazy life while you are young.

You've only got one life - do it while you can.

Be what may - whatever happens.

To see eye to eye on something - to have a similar view about something, to agree.

I got out of the wrong side of the bed this morning - nothing has gone right for me today.

What will be, will be - a fatalistic approach to life.

I wasn't born with a silver spoon - I wasn't born rich. This is also an idiom.

He's got a chip on his shoulder - he holds a grudge.

Old age doesn't come alone - as you get older, you become unwell.

He is a penny short of a shilling - he is stupid.

Like it or lump it - even if you don't like it, you have to endure it.

A chatterbox - someone who talks a lot.

Arrive safe and sound - to arrive safely.

Rebel without a cause - someone who is rebellious and doesn't know what he wants.

Consumer driven - done to satisfy the needs of the consumer.

Dead as a doornail - dead.

A page-turner - this book is a page-turner-interesting.

To be poorly - to be unwell.

On his last legs - dying.

Tie the knot - get married.

I'm stumped - I don't know the answer (from the game of cricket).

Serious setback - major setback.

Penny pincher - someone who saves all his money.

Social butterfly - someone with lots of friends, usually refers to a woman.

Social climber - someone who socialises with people above his social class, usually refers to a woman.

At the end of the day - in conclusion.

To strike up a conversation - to start a conversation with someone you do not know.

Take umbridge - be offended.

Butter wouldn't melt in his/her mouth - when referring to someone, usually a child, who feigns innocence.

To split hairs - to point out very small differences in an argument.

Leanings towards - interested in.

Icing on the cake - best part.

Keep at bay - keep at a distance.

Draws near - gets closer (with reference to time).

Character trait - an aspect of character eg. generosity, friendliness.

Beat around - keep at a distance.

In the nick of time - just in time.

He ticked me off - he made me a little angry.

Hold your horses - don't be so hasty.

To tick off - I ticked off the jobs one by one (I completed the jobs one after the other.

A pet peeve - something that annoys you.

Off the beaten track - somewhere not many people go.

Comfort zone - to be in familiar surroundings.

We are just another brick in the wall. - just another member of the socio-economic structure.

Kicked off - got angry and started shouting.

To lose touch with somebody - to not know where he is and be unable to communicate.

A clip across the ear - a light slap across the side of the head.

To fall for it - to be the victim of a fraud or to believe a lie.

Try to get my head around - try to understand.

To get the hang of it - to gain the ability to do something.

Whisked off to - taken away to - she was whisked off to a castle by a rich prince.

Piqued my interest - raised my interest.

A Heinz 57 - a mongrel (a mixed-breed dog).

To have your ups and downs - the positive and negative experiences of life.

The sun never set on the Union Jack - when the flag was being raised in one part of the world, it was being lowered in another.
