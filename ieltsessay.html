How to Write an IELTS Essay



The internet has brought huge changes, some for the better, others for the worse.

Discuss some of them and state whether, in your opinion, the positive aspects exceed the negative.

The internet has revolutionised the way we communicate, obtain services and search for entertainment. However, I believe there is a negative side of internet use which exceeds the positive. In this essay, I will present and explain my reasons.

Firstly, a deficit of face-to-face communication has arisen from its use. While in the past, people met in social clubs, religious institutions and sporting facilities, most people today are glued to their computer screens and mobile phones. This clearly shows that the internet has had a negative impact on social interraction. If we want to have all the psychological and emotional benefits associated with face-to-face communication, we must spend less time on the internet.

Secondly, the main benefit of internet use is said to be the amount of time saved, particularly on shopping, by lightening-speed information transfer. However, I believe that the lure of the internet actually encourages people to spend more time shopping than they would otherwise do because of all the distractions, such as pop-ups and messages. Furthermore, it deprives people of the valuable exercise they would have received had they had to wander around the malls and market stalls. In this way, the internet can have a negative effect on our health.

In conclusion, internet use has a serious effect on our social lives and our long-term health. These are two of the most important factors for bringing happiness to our lives. Therefore, this negative influence far exceeds any benefit we could possibly receive from surfing the net.

Structure

The essay answers the question exactly as asked, it does not go off topic.

It has an introduction paragraph, followed by two content paragraphs, then a concluding paragraph. Each sentence and paragraph has a purpose, all paragraphs and most sentences are joined to the previous one with a discourse marker.

The essay is only in British English, essays can be written in American English, but should not be mixed.

The word count meets the limit, but does not exceed it. It is inevitable that you will go over a little, but do not exceed, say 265. (there is no exact figure that I am aware of).

Introduction

5 to 10% of total word count.

Paraphrased restatement of question in statement form > The internet has revolutionised the way we communicate, obtain services and search for entertainment.
Position taken, agree or disagree > However, I believe there is a negative side of internet use which exceeds the positive.
Outline of how you will perform the task > In this essay, I will present and explain my reasons.

Main Content

80 to 90% of total word count.

Body Paragraph 1

Statement > Firstly, a deficit of face-to-face communication has arisen from its use.
Explanation of why statement is true/false, why you agree/disagree > While in the past, people met in social clubs, religious institutions and sporting facilities, most people today are glued to their computer screens and mobile phones. This clearly shows that the internet has had a negative impact on social interraction.
Conclusion, suggestion > If we want to have all the psychological and emotional benefits associated with face-to-face communication, we must spend less time on the internet.

Body Paragraph 1

Statement > Secondly, the main benefit of internet use is said to be the amount of time saved, particularly on shopping, by lightening-speed information transfer.
Explanation of why statement is true/false, why you agree/disagree > However, I believe that the lure of the internet actually encourages people to spend more time shopping than they would otherwise do because of all the distractions, such as pop-ups and messages. Furthermore, it deprives people of the valuable exercise they would have received had they had to wander around the malls and market stalls.
Conclusion, suggestion > In this way, the internet can have a negative effect on our health.

Conclusion

5% to 10% of total word count.

Summary of body paragraph conclusions to show question has been answered > In conclusion, internet use has a serious effect on our social lives and our long-term health. These are two of the most important factors for bringing happiness to our lives. Therefore, this negative influence far exceeds any benefit we could possibly receive from surfing the net.

Notes

1. The use of discourse markers: in this essay; firstly; secondly; this clearly shows; however; furthermore; in conclusion.
2. Phrasal verb (a form of idiomatic speech) - glued to
3. Use of passive to show objectivity, but use of active for own opinion.
4. The use of complex grammar - they would have received had they had to...
5. No excessive words or phrases included. - "Perfection is not achieved when you have nothing to add, but when you have nothing to remove" - Antoine de Saint-Exupéry.
