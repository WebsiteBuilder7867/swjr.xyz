Tongue-twisters and Sentences for Pronunciation Practice.

Practice saying the following tongue-twisters to improve your ability to pronounce English words correctly.

    Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers.

    The sixth sick sheik's sixth sheep's sick.

    She sells sea shells on the seashore.

    The rain in Spain falls mainly on the plain.

    How much wood could a woodpecker peck, if a woodpecker could peck wood?

    I am not a pheasant plucker, I'm a pheasant plucker's mate: I'm only plucking pheasants cause the pheasant plucker's late.

   Thirty thousand feathers on a thrush's throat.

   I wish to wash my Irish wrist watch.

   Round and round the rugged rock, the ragged rascal ran.
