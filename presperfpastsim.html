Present Perfect and Past tenses.

Select the correct answer, if more than one answer is correct, select the most appropriate.


1.
Your boss enters the room.
He asks:
A) "Did you finish the task I gave you?"
B) "Did you finish the task I gave you today?"
C) "Have you finished the task I gave you?"
D) "Finished the task I gave you?"

2.
You are in a restaurant, you have eaten your meal.
The waiter asks you:
A) "Have you finished your meal sir?"
B) "Have you been finishing your meal sir?"
C) "Did you finish your meal sir?"
D) "Were you finishing your meal sir?."

3.
A friend asks you:
A) "Did you enjoy school when you were a child?"
B) "Have you enjoyed school when you were a child?"
C) "Did you enjoy school?"
D) "Have you enjoyed the school when you were a child?"

4.
You are walking down the street when you see a thief steal a lady's handbag and run away with it. You hear a whistle and turn around to see a bobby.
He asks you:
A) "Which way was he running?"
B) "Which way have he ran?"
C) "Which way did he ran?"
D) "Which way has he run?"

5.
Choose the grammatically correct sentence.
A) They have not been well recently.
B) They were not well recently
C) They are not well recently
D) They was not well recently.

6.
You are at a New Year's Eve party in Edinburgh and someone offers you a haggis but you don't want it, you say.
A) "Thank you, but I already ate one."
B) "No thank you, I ate one yesterday."
C) "No thanks, I have eat one."
D) "No thank you, I already eat one."
Haggis

7.
You are in Ireland and you find a leprechaun in your room.
You ask:
A) "How long were you here?"
B) "How long did you stay?"
C) "How long have you been here?"
D) "How long was you here?"
Leprechaun

8.
You are in Wales, you ask a Welsh lady for directions to the railway station. She is rude to you.
You sarcastically reply:
A) "Ah, I have found the dragon on the flag!"
B) "Ah, The dragon was on the flag!"
C) "Ah, I found the dragon on the flag!"
D) "Ah, the dragon was not on the flag"
Welsh Flag

9.
Which is the correct Irish saying?
A) A stranger is a friend you met
B) A stranger is someone you meet
C A stranger is someone you would have met
D) A stranger is a friend you have not yet met.

10.
You are in a pub in Belfast. Someone offers you a Guinness.
You reply:
A) "Thank you, I never had a Guinness before."
B) "Thank you, I never had had a Guinness before."
C) "Thank you, I never did have a Guinness before."
D) "Thank you, I have never had a Guinness before."
Guinness

11.
You are at Royal Ascot and want to know if the horses have begun running, you ask:
A) "Did the first race start?"
B) "Was the first race started?"
C) "Has the first race started?"
D) "Has the first race been starting?"
Royal Ascot

12.
Your friend has just returned from watching the Guards at Buckingham Palace and is telling you about what she saw. She says,
A) "They wore black hats and red tunics."
B) "They have been wearing black hats and red tunics."
C) "They has been wearing black hats and red tunics."
D) "They were wearing black hats and red tunics."
Guards at Buckingham Palace

13.
It is 1st March, St David's Day and you are in Cardiff, Wales. You are about to go out for a walk. Your friend asks you if you have put your leek on.
You answer:
A) "I put it on already."
B) "I am putting it on already."
C) "I putted it on already."
D) "I have already put it on."
Leek

14.
You are at an outdoor event in Glasgow, Scotland. A man asks you if you would like to toss the caber.
You reply:
A) "I tossed it."
B) "I have already tossed it."
C) "I tossded it."
D) "I toss it yesterday."
Tossing the Caber

15.
It is late in the evening, you tell your neighbour:
A) "The Royal Family visited my church today."
B) "The Royal Family have visited my church today."
C) "The Royal Family have been visited my church today."
D) "The Royal Family had visited my church today."
The Royal Family

16.
You are watching the tennis at Wimbledon, your friend asks you:
A) “Has he put cream on your strawberries?”
B) “Did he put creams on your strawberries?”
C) “Have he put cream on your strawberries?”
D) “Had he put creams on your strawberries?”
Wimbledon

17.
You are asking your friend whether or not a cricket game was played at Lords yesterday, you ask:
A) "Have they played cricket yesterday?"
B) "Were they playing cricket yesterday?"
C) "Did they play cricket yesterday?"
D) "Were they played cricket yesterday?"
Lords

18.
You are at Heathrow airport, but realise
you have left your passport in the hotel.
You say:
A) "I have lefted my passport in the hotel"
B) "I have leaved my passport in the hotel."
C) "I left my passport in the hotel"
D) "I have left my passport in the hotel"

19.
You are being interviewed for British Citizenship in the UK. A government official asks you:
A) "How long have you lived in the UK?"
B) "How long did you lived in the UK?"
C) "How long did you live in the UK?"
D) "How long you been living in the UK?"

20.
You are telling an immigration officer why you wish to become a British citizen. You tell him:
A) "I always have wanted to be a British football hooligan."
B) "I always had wanted to be a Brexiteer."
C) "I had want to always join the British Army and visit hot countries as an unwanted guest."
D) "I have always loved Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth the Second."

Football Hooligan = someone who fights at football matches. Brexiteer = someone who supports Brexit.
